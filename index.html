This document explains the structure of plant cells and how they differ from animal cells. Plant cells contain chloroplasts which allow them to perform photosynthesis and produce energy from sunlight.

Photosynthesis occurs mainly in the leaves of plants and requires sunlight, carbon dioxide, and water. The chlorophyll inside chloroplasts absorbs light energy which is used to produce glucose and oxygen.

Animal cells do not contain chloroplasts because animals obtain their energy by consuming food rather than producing it.
